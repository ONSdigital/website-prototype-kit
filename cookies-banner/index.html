Crime in England and Wales: year ending December 2018

Crime against households and adults, also including data on crime experienced by children, and crimes against businesses and society.

Main points

  • Overall levels of crime continue to fall, with no significant change to the long-term trend.
  • Police reported a 6% increase in offences involving knives or sharp instruments, a 6% increase in the number of homicides, and a 2% decrease in the number of police recorded offences involving firearms.
  • 8% increase in overall theft offences estimated by the Crime Survey for England and Wales (CSEW), mainly driven by a 20% increase in “other household theft”.
  • Police reported a 6% increase in offences involving knives or sharp instruments, a 6% increase in the number of homicides, and a 2% decrease in the number of police recorded offences involving firearms.
  • Estimated 2% increase in vehicle offences reported by the police, due largely to a 9% increase in the subcategory of “theft or unauthorised taking of a motor vehicle”.
  • Police reported an 11% increase in robbery, and a 3% decrease in burglaries.
! Warning An increase in the number of crimes recorded by the police does not necessarily mean the level of crime has increased. See the Strengths and limitations section for more information.
